## Card 1: Bauhaus

### Claim 1
- **Claim:** Walter Gropius founded the Bauhaus in Weimar, Germany in 1919.
- **Source link(s):** https://www.bauhaus.de/en/
- **Confidence:** High
- **Notes:** The Bauhaus-Archiv website, that is based in Berlin, confirms Walter Gropius founded the school in Weimar in 1919. It later moved to Dessau in 1925 and Berlin in 1932 before closing in 1933.

## Card 2: Swiss Style

### Claim 1
- **Claim:** Helvetica was released in 1957 and became the most popular typeface of Swiss Style.
- **Source link(s):** https://www.zuerich.com/en/art-culture/zurich-avantgarde/swiss-graphics-typography , https://letterformarchive.org/news/legacies-of-swiss-style-tyografische-monatsblatter/
- **Confidence:** High
- **Notes:** Helvetica was originally named Neue Haas Grotesk and was designed by Max Miedinger with Eduard Hoffmann at the Haas Type Foundry in Switzerland in 1957. It was later renamed to Helvetica in 1960 for international marketing.

## Card 3: Grunge

### Claim 1
- **Claim:** Rooted in Postmodernism, grunge argued that emotional resonance, not optical clarity, defines whether design communicates.
- **Source link(s):** https://www.ted.com/talks/david_carson_design_and_discovery
- **Confidence:** High
- **Notes:** David Carson's 2003 TED talk "Design and Discovery" argued that legibility and communication are not the same thing. Design that is technically readable but emotionally flat fails to communicate, while design that disrupts conventional clarity can still resonate.
